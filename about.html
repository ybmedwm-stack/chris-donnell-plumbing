Our Story

Founded 2002 · Incorporated 2003 · Listed on the Blue Book since 2010

Chris Donnell started this company in 2002 with a simple idea: be the plumber who answers the phone, shows up on time, and does the work the way you'd want it done in your own house. More than twenty years later, that idea still drives the shop — and most of our customers still come from word of mouth.

"He responds right away, his services are reasonably priced and he performs consistently high quality work."
— Jody S., verified BBB review

We're based at 3 Scituate Trail in York, Maine. We're a small team — Chris and his crew — and we keep it that way on purpose. It means the person who quotes your job is usually the person doing the work, and the work doesn't get handed off to someone who's never seen the inside of your house.

The business serves residential and commercial customers across York County and the New Hampshire Seacoast, from York Beach and Cape Neddick all the way down to Portsmouth and Dover. We've installed septic systems, swapped boilers in February, fixed sinks the day after Christmas, and handled the unglamorous emergencies that don't make for great stories but matter a lot when they're happening to you.

How We Work

You call, we answer. When you dial (207) 363-1900, you reach the shop — not a national call center or a lead-routing service. That's a deliberate choice. A lot of plumbing companies have grown into systems where the person on the phone doesn't actually know the people doing the work. We're not that.

Estimates are estimates, not opening bids. If we quote you a price, that's the price. We don't pad our estimates and we don't surprise you with line items on the invoice. If we hit something unexpected during the job — old houses do that — we call you before we spend your money.

We do the work we'd do at home. Cleaner solder joints, neater pipe runs, careful attention to the parts of the job nobody will ever see. Code-compliant means the floor, not the ceiling. If we wouldn't sign off on it in our own basement, we won't sign off on it in yours.

We stand behind it. Our work comes with our name on it, and our name is on every truck and every invoice. If something we did isn't right, we come back and make it right. That's how a local business stays a local business for twenty years.
